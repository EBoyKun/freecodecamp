Bob Marley

More than a singer, a life teacher

A time line of Bob Marley's life:

  • 1945 - Bob Marley is born on February 6th in Nine Miles, St Ann's, Jamaica.
  • 1961 - Bob Marley cuts his first record at the age of 16 with Leslie Kong, a Jamaican music producer.
  • 1962 - Jamaica gains independence from the United Kingdom, and Bob Marley begins to focus on his music career.
  • 1972 - Bob Marley signs with Island Records and releases the album "Catch a Fire," which brings international recognition to reggae music.
  • 1974 - The album "Natty Dread" is released, featuring the hit songs "No Woman, No Cry" and "Them Belly Full (But We Hungry)."
  • 1976 - Bob Marley survives an assassination attempt at his home in Jamaica, just days before a scheduled peace concert.
  • 1977 - The album "Exodus" is released, which includes the iconic songs "Jamming" and "One Love/People Get Ready."
  • 1980 - Bob Marley performs his final concert at the Stanley Theater in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.
  • 1981 - Bob Marley passes away on May 11th in Miami, Florida, after a battle with cancer.
